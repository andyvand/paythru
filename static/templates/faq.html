Frequently Asked Questions

New to Bitcoin? Learn more about how Bitcoin is used to send money over the internet at We Use Coins or get started now by choosing a wallet. Users in the United States can buy bitcoins with Coinbase.

How does it work?

The goal of ${sitename} is to foster Bitcoin adoption through increased use and public awareness. ${sitename} takes the approach that a URI can act as its own authority and "publish" a Bitcoin address. By connecting those published addresses into a single, simple interface, ${sitename} can serve as an internet bitcoin directory. If we know the Bitcoin address for a URI (either through a published protocol or claimed through proof-of-control), we'll report it. If not, ${sitename} will generate a deposit address to collect funds until the receiver claims them. An account balance must be at least enough to cover the network fee (0.0001 BTC) before funds can be sent.

How does the person know I sent them money?

Currently, ${sitename} uses email as the sole notification mechanism, however, I'm planning to add other services such as Twitter and SMS.

What happens if no one claims the funds I sent?

There is a 2016 block (configurable) limit which will trigger a refund to the first address on the "input" to the transaction. More specifically, the "input" is the previous transaction's outputs, so non-simple transactions may not work as expected. It's not perfect, but it's the best that can be done with the current protocol. It would be much preferrable if the person publishes thier own Bitcoin address :)

Are there fees?

${sitename} charges no fees, although we deduct 0.0001 BTC when forwarding funds to encourage timely processing by the network. This is standard in many clients and can be adjusted as needed. Because of the network fee, account balances must be greater than the fee to forward. The account balance can be seen on the blockchain.

This is new, how can I trust it?

Trust is built over time, so I hope the Bitcoin community finds this useful and builds off of it. Until then, all I can suggest is that you go out and try it. Send a few bitcents to your friends; we'll handle the notification. Or register your website with a supported publishing method. We'll pick that up and ${sitename} will report it dutifully. Once your Bitcoin address is published, ${sitename} can't even touch the transaction.

What happens if someone sends Bitcoins to old Bitcoin address?

If funds are sent to the old ${sitename}-generated address even after you've published your Bitcoin address, we'll attempt to forward that transaction to the published address. As mentioned elsewhere, the balance must be more than the fee of 0.0001 BTC

There are bitcoins stuck at the temporary ${sitename} deposit address!

A single confirmation is required for funds to be forwarded. Additionally, if funds are sent to the old ${sitename}-generated address even after you've claimed your URI, we'll attempt to forward the transaction. If funds are STILL stuck at an old address for any reason, re-claiming your URI (even to your own same address) should re-trigger the sweep functionality out of our wallet and into yours. If none of these unstick it for you, please contact me for manual processing (and bug hunting!)
